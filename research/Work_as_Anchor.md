# Work as an Anchor: Research Overview

A collection of data on how humans use employment as a source of identity, structure, meaning, and connection — and what happens when that anchor weakens.
Last updated: March 2026. Sources verified via live web search where marked ✓.

---

## What Work Provides (Beyond Income)

Employment serves not only as a source of income but as a key contributor to mental health through psychosocial benefits:
- **Time structure** — the day has a shape
- **Social contact** — coworkers as a primary community
- **Purpose and status** — a role in the world
- **Personal development** — a sense of growth
- **Identity** — "what do you do?" is often "who are you?"

Research consistently shows that **roughly two-thirds of the psychological cost of unemployment** comes not from lost income but from the loss of these non-financial functions. The money matters less than the anchor.

## Engagement — How Attached Are People to Their Work?

### Gallup State of the Global Workplace (2024) ✓
- **62%** of employees globally are *not engaged* — doing the minimum, psychologically detached
- **15%** are *actively disengaged* — opposed to their employer's goals
- Only **21–23%** of workers globally are engaged — down from 23% to 21% in the latest report
- US engagement: **31%** — the **lowest in a decade**
- **90%** of UK employees are "quiet quitting" (doing minimum required)
- Combined cost of disengagement and active disengagement: **$8.9 trillion/year — 9% of global GDP**
- https://www.gallup.com/workplace/349484/state-of-the-global-workplace.aspx

### Meaning at Work
- Only **30%** of US workers report a strong sense of mission or purpose at work — down from 38% pre-pandemic
- 70% of US adults say their job or career provides at least *some* meaning (Pew, 2017) — but the depth is shallow
- **93%** of workers value wellbeing as much as salary
- **92%** say it's important to work for an organisation that values their psychological wellbeing
- https://www.weforum.org/stories/2024/09/young-people-meaningful-work/

## Burnout — When the Anchor Becomes a Weight

### Burnout Statistics (2024–2025) ✓
- **59%** of US employees reported burnout in 2024
- **82%** of employees are at risk of burnout (broader measure)
- Gen Z and millennials hit peak burnout at **age 25** — 17 years earlier than the average American (age 42)
- Women: **59%** report burnout vs. men at **46%**
- **45%** of workers aged 18–25 report feeling overworked
- By profession: physicians 48%, nurses **62%**, female teachers **63%**
- https://blog.theinterviewguys.com/workplace-burnout-in-2025-research-report/

### Workaholism ✓
- **76%** of US workers consider themselves at least "somewhat workaholic"
- **45%** identify as a full workaholic
- Workaholics define personal value through titles, accomplishments, and recognition
- The pattern: each success offers brief relief before pressure re-emerges — work as self-medication
- https://clockify.me/workaholism-facts

## Unemployment — When the Anchor Is Removed

### Mental Health Impact of Unemployment ✓
- Unemployment consistently linked to: depression, anxiety, psychosomatic complaints, reduced self-efficacy
- The psychological cost is **larger than the financial cost** — roughly 2/3 of total wellbeing impact
- Longer unemployment duration = larger psychological damage; recovery after re-employment is often incomplete
- The effect is global — consistent across income levels and countries
- Social isolation is a direct consequence: unemployed people report lower self-esteem and lack of social support
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11672120/

## AI Displacement — The Coming Unwinding

### Scale of Risk ✓
- **980 million jobs** worldwide face high disruption risk from AI in the near term
- **92 million jobs** projected to be displaced by 2030; 170 million new ones expected to emerge — but the transition is brutal
- Unemployment among 20–30 year olds in tech-exposed occupations has risen **~3 percentage points** since early 2025
- Workers aged 18–24 are **129% more likely** than those 65+ to fear AI will make their job obsolete
- **79%** of employed women in the US work in jobs at high risk of automation vs. 58% of men
- https://www.weforum.org/stories/2025/08/the-overlooked-global-risk-of-the-ai-precariat/

### The Identity Crisis ✓
- Research on workers facing AI displacement shows: anxiety, burnout, and a **sense of disorientation** — loss of identity, not just income
- The emerging concept: the **"AI precariat"** — workers in permanent instability, unable to anchor to any role long enough to build identity around it
- When millions lose not just income but the anchor that work provides, the social consequences are structural

## The Paradox

| What's true | Data |
|---|---|
| Work is the primary source of meaning for most people | 70% of US adults derive meaning from work (Pew) |
| Most people are not actually engaged in their work | 62% globally disengaged (Gallup 2024) |
| Burnout is near-universal | 59–82% of US workers affected |
| Unemployment causes psychological damage beyond income loss | 2/3 of unemployment's cost is psychological |
| Work identity is being destabilised by AI | 980M jobs at high disruption risk |
| Younger workers are most vulnerable | Peak burnout at 25; 129% more likely to fear AI displacement |

**The pattern:** People have concentrated their identity, meaning, social life, and daily structure into work — and that anchor is simultaneously making them miserable (burnout, disengagement) and becoming less reliable (automation, precarity). There is no obvious replacement being built.
