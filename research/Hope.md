# Hope, Optimism & Belonging: Research Overview

A collection of data on how humans feel about the future — and how much they feel they belong.
Last updated: March 2026. Sources verified via live web search where marked ✓.

---

## Global Optimism

### Gallup International End of Year Survey (2024) ✓
- **41%** of people across 37 countries say things will be *better* in 2025
- **24%** say things will be *worse*
- **29%** expect no change
- Global median of adults classified as "thriving" in 2024: **33%** (across 142 countries) — improving
- But in wealthy liberal democracies, thriving has *dipped*: North America (49%), Western Europe (42%)
- https://www.gallup-international.com/survey-results-and-news/survey-result/global-poll-shows-people-to-generally-be-happy-and-optimistic-for-2025

### Ipsos Predictions Survey (2025) ✓
- **71%** of respondents across 33 countries feel optimistic that 2025 will be better than 2024
- Most optimistic: Indonesia (90%), China, Brazil, Kenya
- Least optimistic: France (50%), Japan (38%)
- **79%** believe prices will increase faster than incomes in 2025
- Fewer than **1 in 3** believe the next year will be one of economic prosperity
- https://www.ipsos.com/en-us/ipsos-predictions-2025

## United States — Optimism Declining

### Gallup U.S. Wellbeing (2025) ✓
- Americans rating their future lives highly: **59.2% in 2025** — the **lowest ever recorded**
- Since 2021 peak: future life ratings have fallen **9.1 percentage points**
- Adults classified as "thriving": dropped to **48%** in Q4 2025 — down from 59.2% high in June 2021
- Worker thriving: **46%** in Q4 2025 — lowest in Gallup's trended data
- **American optimism at a record low** as of early 2026
- https://news.gallup.com/poll/702125/american-optimism-slumps-record-low.aspx
- https://news.gallup.com/poll/692816/people-globally-living-better-lives.aspx

## Youth — Hope and Fear

### PwC Global Youth Outlook (2024) ✓
- Youth in Kenya, China, and Brazil are the most optimistic about their futures
- Youth in Germany, UK, and France are among the least — only ~**1 in 4 German youth** expect their living conditions to improve
- Top youth priorities: education, skills, workforce readiness
- Youth feel only "somewhat" involved in decisions about issues they care about (2.29 / 5)
- https://www.pwc.com/gx/en/industries/government-public-services/global-youth-outlook.html

### Climate Anxiety (Lancet / multiple sources) ✓
- **~20% of young Americans** (16–24) are afraid to have children due to climate worry
- That figure rises to **30%+** among those who have personally experienced a severe weather event
- A 2024 Lancet survey of ~16,000 young people: **62%** tried to talk to others about climate change — **58%** felt ignored or dismissed
- Three dimensions of eco-anxiety identified in research: *helplessness in the present*, *hopelessness about the future*, *acute stress from lived disasters*
- https://time.com/7280989/climate-anxiety-mental-health-young-people/
- https://www.thelancet.com/journals/lanplh/article/PIIS2542-5196(21)00278-3/fulltext

## Belonging — A Crisis Alongside Loneliness

### UK Belonging Survey (2024) ✓
- Largest national poll on belonging ever conducted in the UK
- **1 in 3 older people** don't feel they belong to their neighbourhood
- Young women (18–24), renters, and people with disabilities feel the sharpest lack of belonging
- A new national organisation launched specifically to address the **"crisis of belonging"**
- https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2024/03/27/2852939/0/en/A-crisis-of-Belonging-revealed

### Belonging Barometer — USA ✓
- **64%** of Americans reported non-belonging in the workplace
- **67%** reported non-belonging in the nation
- **74%** reported non-belonging in their local community
- **94%** of workers say it's "very or somewhat important" that their workplace feels like somewhere they belong
- https://www.americanimmigrationcouncil.org/report/the-belonging-barometer/

### College Students (NCHA, Fall 2024) ✓
- **48.2%** of surveyed college students screened positive for loneliness

## The Pattern

| Group | Feeling | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Global adults | 41% expect things to improve | Gallup Int'l, 2024 |
| American adults | Optimism at all-time low | Gallup US, 2025 |
| Western Europe / North America | Wellbeing dipping vs. global trend | Gallup, 2024 |
| Young Americans (16–24) | 20% afraid to have children | PNAS study, 2025 |
| Young Europeans | Only 1 in 4 German youth expect improvement | PwC, 2024 |
| Americans at work | 64% feel they don't belong | Belonging Barometer |
| Americans in community | 74% feel they don't belong locally | Belonging Barometer |
| College students | 48.2% screen positive for loneliness | NCHA, 2024 |

**The divergence:** globally, optimism is slowly recovering — but in wealthy, connected, technologically advanced nations (US, UK, Germany, France, Japan), hope is declining. The places with the most infrastructure have the least sense of belonging.

## Notable Framings

- *"A crisis of belonging"* — UK national coalition, 2024
- *"American optimism slumps to record low"* — Gallup headline, 2025/2026
- Youth feel **ignored** when they speak up about what worries them (58%, Lancet 2024)
- The countries *most optimistic* about the future (Kenya, Indonesia, Brazil) are largely not the ones driving the technological transformation
