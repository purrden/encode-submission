# Public Attitudes Toward Technology & AI

A collection of data on how humans feel about technological advancement and artificial intelligence.
Last updated: March 2026. Sources verified via live web search where marked ✓.

---

## Global AI Sentiment

### KPMG / University of Melbourne — Global Trust Study (2025) ✓
- **48,000+ people surveyed across 47 countries** (Nov 2024 – Jan 2025)
- **66%** use AI regularly — but only **46%** are willing to trust AI systems
- **83%** believe AI will result in wide-ranging benefits
- **70%** believe regulation is needed for AI oversight
- Compared to 2022 (pre-ChatGPT): people have become *less trusting* and *more worried* as adoption has increased — trust and usage are moving in opposite directions
- https://kpmg.com/xx/en/our-insights/ai-and-technology/trust-attitudes-and-use-of-ai.html

### Stanford AI Index Report (2025) ✓
- Globally, those who see AI as more beneficial than harmful: **52% (2022) → 55% (2024)**
- Striking regional divide:
  - **Optimistic**: China (83%), Indonesia (80%), Thailand (77%)
  - **Skeptical**: Canada (40%), USA (39%), Netherlands (36%)
- Optimism is growing fastest in previously skeptical nations (UK +8%, Germany +10%, USA +4%)
- **60%** globally agree AI will change how they do their job in the next 5 years
- **36%** believe AI will replace their jobs in the next 5 years
- Confidence that AI companies protect personal data: fell from **50% (2023) → 47% (2024)**
- https://hai.stanford.edu/ai-index/2025-ai-index-report/public-opinion

## United States

### Pew Research Center (2025) ✓
- **50%** of Americans are more *concerned* than excited about AI — up from 37% in 2021
- **57%** rate the societal risks of AI as high; only **25%** say the benefits are high
- **57%** of US workers feel worried about future AI use in the workplace vs. 44% hopeful
- AI experts vs. public on positive 20-year outlook: **56% of experts vs. 17% of public**
- Expert vs. public on being "more excited than concerned": **47% of experts vs. 11% of public**
- **57% of public** (vs. 37% of experts) fear AI will cause loss of human connection
- **56% of public** (vs. 25% of experts) extremely/very concerned about AI eliminating jobs
- Key summary: **the gap between expert optimism and public anxiety is enormous**
- https://www.pewresearch.org/internet/2025/04/03/how-the-us-public-and-ai-experts-view-artificial-intelligence/
- https://www.pewresearch.org/social-trends/2025/02/25/u-s-workers-are-more-worried-than-hopeful-about-future-ai-use-in-the-workplace/

## Edelman Trust Barometer — Technology Sector ✓

### 2024 Findings
- Trust in tech businesses: **76%** — but trust in AI specifically: **50%** — a 26-point gap
- Nearly **2x more people** are concerned about AI privacy threats than about job impacts
- Innovation is now seen as a **risk factor** for trust — not just a benefit
- https://www.edelman.com/trust/2024/trust-barometer/special-report-tech-sector

### 2025 Findings
- **~1 in 2 respondents** are skeptical of business use of AI
- China is **40 points more trusting** of AI than the US
- Five of the 10 largest economies are among the least trusting nations: Japan (37), Germany (41), UK (43), USA (47), France (48) — these are trust index scores, not percentages
- Society has moved from *fear → polarization → grievance*
- https://www.edelman.com/news-awards/2024-edelman-trust-barometer

## Technology & Loneliness — The Connection

### Social Media and Loneliness (2025 research) ✓
- **63%** of people on social media report feelings of loneliness
- Upper 25% of social media usage frequency: **more than twice as likely** to experience loneliness
- Nuance: not all platforms equal — Reddit and dating apps associated with higher loneliness; WhatsApp associated with *lower* loneliness
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12220285/

### Teen Mental Health & Social Media ✓
- **45%** of teens say they spend too much time on social media (2025) — up from 36% in 2022
- Among heavy teen users: **41% rate their mental health as poor or very poor** vs. 23% among light users
- Increasing daily social media use from ~7 min to ~74 min in 9–10 year olds: **35% jump in depressive symptoms** after 3 years
- Nearly **10%** of teen girls (13–17) say social media hurt their mental health vs. 6% of boys
- **11%** of adolescents showed signs of problematic social media use (WHO Europe, 2024)
- Globally ~**210 million** social media users may suffer addiction-level use (4.7% of 4.48B users)
- https://sqmagazine.co.uk/social-media-mental-health-statistics/

### "Digital Loneliness" — Emerging Concept ✓
- Researchers are now proposing "digital loneliness" as a distinct diagnostic category in psychiatry
- The paradox: technology is simultaneously the primary social infrastructure and a driver of isolation
- https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychiatry/articles/10.3389/fpsyt.2025.1630275/full

## The Core Tension

| What people feel | Data |
|---|---|
| AI is useful | 66% use it regularly (KPMG 2025) |
| AI is not trustworthy | Only 46% willing to trust it (KPMG 2025) |
| Tech connects us | WhatsApp → lower loneliness |
| Tech isolates us | Heavy social media → 2x loneliness risk |
| AI benefits exist | 83% believe it broadly (KPMG 2025) |
| AI risks are high | 57% of Americans rate societal risk as high (Pew 2025) |
| Experts are optimistic | 56% see positive 20-year outlook (Pew 2025) |
| Public is not | Only 17% of Americans share that view (Pew 2025) |

**The pattern:** People are adopting technology faster than they trust it, and the humans most qualified to evaluate it (experts) are significantly more optimistic than the humans living with it (the public). Meanwhile, the technology designed to connect people is measurably increasing loneliness.
