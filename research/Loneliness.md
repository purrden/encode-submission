# Loneliness & Human Connection: Research Overview

A collection of data on how human connection and loneliness have changed over time, globally.
Last updated: March 2026. Sources verified via live web search where marked ✓.

---

## Global

### WHO Commission on Social Connection (2023–2024) ✓
- **1 in 6 people** worldwide experienced loneliness between 2014–2023
- Loneliness and social isolation linked to **871,000 deaths per year** — about 100 deaths per hour
- Most affected: adolescents (20.9% aged 13–17) and young adults (17.4% aged 18–29)
- In lower-income countries: nearly **1 in 4** people affected
- Only a handful of countries have national strategies: Denmark, Finland, Germany, Netherlands, Sweden, England, Scotland, Wales, Japan
- Full report: https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/978240112360
- Commission overview: https://www.who.int/groups/commission-on-social-connection

### Gallup World Poll (2023)
- ~**1 in 4 people globally** report feeling very lonely (note: Gallup frames this around lack of social support — slightly different metric than WHO's)
- US tracks worse than most peer nations on several social connection metrics

## United States

### US Surgeon General Advisory (2023)
- ~**50% of American adults** reported measurable loneliness *before* COVID-19
- Americans have **3 fewer hours/week** of in-person social interaction vs. two decades earlier
- Social isolation increases premature death risk by ~26% — comparable to smoking 15 cigarettes/day
- Raises risk of heart disease by 29%, stroke by 32%, dementia by ~50%
- https://www.hhs.gov/surgeon-general/priorities/connection/index.html

### Cigna U.S. Loneliness Index (2018–2020)
- 2018: ~54% of Americans sometimes or always feel lonely
- 2020: rose to **61%** — highest recorded
- Gen Z (18–22) scored as the **loneliest generation** — lonelier than adults 72+
- Heavy social media users were *more* lonely, not less
- Note: Cigna discontinued the annual index after 2020 under that name
- https://newsroom.cigna.com/loneliness-in-america

### Harvard "Making Caring Common" (2021)
- **36%** of all Americans felt serious loneliness
- **61%** of young adults (18–25) — the most striking inversion (expected: elderly)
- **51%** of mothers with young children reported serious loneliness
- https://mcc.gse.harvard.edu/reports/loneliness-in-america

### General Social Survey — NORC (longitudinal, since 1972) ✓
- 1990: ~3% of Americans had **zero close friends**
- 2021: ~**12%** — a 4x increase over 30 years
- Average close confidants: ~3 (1985) → ~2 (2011), with growing share reporting 0–1
- https://gss.norc.org

### American Time Use Survey — BLS
- Time alone increased steadily across all demographics through the 2010s
- Teens and young adults spend roughly **an extra hour per day alone** vs. early 2000s equivalents
- https://www.bls.gov/tus/

### Jean Twenge / iGen Research
- Sharp inflection point: **2012** — when smartphone adoption crossed ~50% among teens
- Teen loneliness, depression, and social isolation all began rising sharply from that year
- Time with friends in person dropped ~**40%** between 2007–2019 for teens
- Book: *iGen* (2017); follow-up papers in *Journal of Adolescence* and *Psychological Science*

### Holt-Lunstad Meta-Analysis (2015, 2017)
- 70 studies, ~3.4 million people
- Social isolation increases mortality risk by **29%**, loneliness by **26%**, living alone by **32%**
- Brigham Young University — most-cited research in the field; underpins the Surgeon General's framing

## United Kingdom

### Jo Cox Commission on Loneliness (2017)
- **9 million people** in the UK (of ~67m) often or always feel lonely
- UK became the first country to appoint a **Minister for Loneliness** (2018)
- https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/loneliness-annual-report

### Office for National Statistics (ONS)
- Formal loneliness tracking since 2016
- COVID-era data: ~7.4% of adults (3.7 million people) felt lonely "often or always" (2020)
- https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/wellbeing/bulletins/measuringlonelinessguidanceforuse

## Europe

### EU-Wide Survey — Joint Research Centre (2022) ✓
- First-ever EU-wide loneliness survey, 25,000+ respondents across 27 countries
- **~13%** of EU citizens feel lonely most or all of the time
- Highest: Ireland (20%+), Luxembourg, Bulgaria, Greece
- Lowest: Netherlands, Czech Republic, Croatia, Austria (all below 10%)
- https://joint-research-centre.ec.europa.eu/projects-and-activities/survey-methods-and-analysis-centre/loneliness/loneliness-prevalence-eu_en

### Youth Loneliness in Europe — Bertelsmann Stiftung (2024) ✓
- **57% of young Europeans** (18–35) are moderately or severely lonely
- Germany: 51% of young adults at least moderately lonely (12% severely)
- Loneliness strongly correlated with low educational attainment
- https://www.bertelsmann-stiftung.de/en/our-projects/next-generation-and-society/project-news/a-comparison-of-youth-loneliness-in-europe-in-2024

### Pre-Pandemic European Baseline (European Social Survey)
- 8.6% of European adults suffer from frequent loneliness
- 20.8% from social isolation
- Eastern Europe records highest prevalence of both
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12166141/

### France — Fondation de France (annual since 2010) ✓
- **12% of French people in total relational isolation** (2024 data, published Jan 2025)
- **1 in 3** French people in "relational fragility" (only 0–1 active social networks)
- 83% of isolated people suffer from their situation — up 4 points since 2020
- 14th annual edition published January 2025; full PDF available
- French term: *isolement social* / *solitude*
- https://www.fondationdefrance.org/fr/cat-personnes-vulnerables/etude-solitudes-2025

### Germany
- ~15–20% of Germans report frequent loneliness (DIW — Deutsches Institut für Wirtschaftsforschung)
- Young people (18–35) increasingly overrepresented — shift from elderly stereotype
- German term: *Einsamkeit*

### Netherlands
- CBS (Centraal Bureau voor de Statistiek) tracks loneliness annually
- Among lowest in EU per JRC 2022 survey (below 10% frequent loneliness)
- https://www.cbs.nl

## Japan

### Hikikomori — Cabinet Office Survey (2023) ✓
- **1.46 million people** (2% of those aged 15–64) living in social withdrawal
- Age 15–39: 2.05% classified as hikikomori; age 40–64: 2.02% — phenomenon now spans all adult ages
- Most common trigger: quitting jobs; COVID-19 cited by ~18–20% of respondents
- Definition: not leaving home (or only for personal errands) for 6+ months
- https://medicalxpress.com/news/2023-04-japan-govt-survey-million-recluses.html

### Minister for Loneliness (2021)
- Japan appointed its own **Minister of Loneliness** in 2021 — modeled on the UK's initiative
- Japan's suicide rate rose in 2020 for the first time in 11 years — women +15%
- One of only a handful of countries with national legislation on loneliness (per WHO 2024)

## South Korea

### Godoksa — "Lonely Death" Statistics ✓
- **3,661 lonely deaths** recorded in 2023 — up from 3,559 in 2022 (3rd consecutive annual rise)
- Men in their 50s and 60s account for **54%** of all lonely deaths
- Men die lonely deaths at nearly **5x the rate** of women
- 41.4% of lonely death victims were welfare recipients
- Single-person households: 7.16M (2021) → 7.5M (2022) → **7.82M (2023)**
- https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/www/nation/2024/10/281_384439.html

### Government Response
- Seoul announced **$327 million** (451.3 billion won) over 5 years to "create a city where no one is lonely"
- Initiatives: 24/7 loneliness hotline, in-person counselors, online platform
- **Lonely Death Prevention Act** enacted 2021
- https://edition.cnn.com/2024/10/24/asia/south-korea-loneliness-deaths-intl-hnk

## Australia

### Ending Loneliness Together ✓
- ~**1 in 4 Australians** feel lonely — consistent with global patterns
- Young adults (18–25) most affected, ahead of elderly
- https://endingloneliness.com.au

### Australian Institute of Health and Welfare (AIHW)
- Social isolation tracked as a health determinant
- Loneliness correlated with higher rates of mental illness, emergency department presentations, and reduced life expectancy
- https://www.aihw.gov.au

## Brazil

### ELSI-Brazil (Brazilian Longitudinal Study of Aging) ✓
- Among adults 50+: **16.8% always lonely**, 31.7% sometimes lonely, 51.5% never lonely
- Pre-pandemic overall prevalence: **33.1%** — decreased during pandemic (counterintuitively)
- Risk factors: depression, living alone, low education, female sex, poor self-rated health
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10494680/

### Structural Context
- Rapid urbanization eroding extended family cohabitation and traditional community structures
- Catholic Church attendance in sharp decline — historically a primary social connector
- Academic field growing; key term: *solidão* (loneliness) / *isolamento social*
- https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12334170/

## China

- "Empty nest elderly" (*空巢老人, kōng cháo lǎorén*) — estimated **100 million+** people
- Legacy of one-child policy: generations of only children with weaker peer networks
- "Lying flat" (*躺平, tǎng píng*) movement — partly a response to social pressure and disconnection among youth
- No comprehensive national loneliness index equivalent to Western surveys; academic literature growing

## Notes on Methodology

- "Loneliness" is subjective; the **UCLA Loneliness Scale** (20-item) is the most validated instrument
- Cross-national comparisons are difficult — cultural norms around reporting differ significantly
- WHO and Gallup figures differ: WHO says "1 in 6", Gallup says "1 in 4" — different definitions and methodologies
- COVID introduced a confound — some spikes may partially normalize; notably, Brazil saw *decreased* reported loneliness during lockdowns
- Most consistent finding across all methodologies: **young adults (18–25) are now the loneliest cohort** — inverting decades of assumption that elderly people are most at risk
- **2012** appears as a recurring inflection point in Western data — correlates with smartphone adoption crossing 50% among teens
