# Headlines for the PURR Pitch

---

## On Loneliness

- Americans with zero close friends: **3% (1990) → 12% (2021)** — a 4x increase in 30 years
- **61% of young adults** (18–25) feel serious loneliness — the loneliest cohort, not the elderly
- **1 in 6 people** worldwide experienced loneliness over the last decade (WHO, 2023)
- Loneliness causes **871,000 deaths per year** — about 100 deaths per hour (WHO, 2024)
- Loneliness is as deadly as **smoking 15 cigarettes a day** (Holt-Lunstad)
- Teen time with friends in person dropped **~40%** between 2007 and 2019
- **2012** is the inflection point — when smartphones crossed 50% teen adoption, everything got worse
- South Korea passed a **Lonely Death Prevention Act** in 2021; **3,661 people** died alone in 2023
- Seoul is spending **$327 million** to become "a city where no one is lonely"
- Japan has a **Minister for Loneliness**. So does the UK. Both modeled national policy on the crisis.
- **57% of young Europeans** (18–35) are moderately or severely lonely (Bertelsmann, 2024)

---

## On Technology & AI

- **66% of people use AI regularly** — but only **46% trust it** (KPMG, 2025)
- Trust and usage are moving in **opposite directions**
- **57% of Americans** fear AI will cause **loss of human connection** (Pew, 2025)
- Only **17% of Americans** believe AI will have a positive impact over the next 20 years — vs. **56% of AI experts**
- The gap between expert optimism and public anxiety is **enormous and growing**
- **"Innovation has become a new risk factor for trust"** — Edelman Trust Barometer, 2024
- Heavy social media users are **more than twice as likely** to feel lonely
- **41% of heavy teen social media users** rate their mental health as poor — vs. 23% of light users
- Researchers are now proposing **"digital loneliness"** as a distinct psychiatric diagnosis (Frontiers, 2025)

---

## The One Sentence That Connects Both

The technology built to connect us is measurably making us lonelier —
and the people who know it best are the least worried about it.
