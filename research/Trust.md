# Trust Between Humans: Research Overview

A collection of data on how much people trust each other — interpersonal, institutional, and societal.
Last updated: March 2026. Sources verified via live web search where marked ✓.

---

## The Long View — Interpersonal Trust Over Time

### Our World in Data / World Values Survey (longitudinal) ✓
- US General Social Survey tracking trust since 1972: **people in the US trust each other less today than 40 years ago**
- The integrated EVS-WVS time-series covers 1981–2022 across dozens of countries
- Short-term fluctuations exist, but the long-term direction in Western nations is downward
- https://ourworldindata.org/trust

### Country Comparisons — "Most people can be trusted"
- **Norway, Sweden, Denmark**: 60%+ say most people can be trusted
- **France, Italy**: ~26%
- **USA**: ~34–55% depending on survey methodology and year
- **Colombia, Brazil, Peru**: as low as 5–10%
- Strong positive correlation between trust and GDP per capita (but causality unclear)
- https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/self-reported-trust-attitudes

### Ipsos Global Survey ✓
- Only **30% of adults globally** say most people can be trusted
- https://www.ipsos.com/en/interpersonal-trust-across-the-world

## United States

### Pew Research — Americans' Trust in Each Other (2025) ✓
- **55%** of US adults say most people can be trusted — **44%** say they can't
- Long-run GSS data: share saying "most people can be trusted" fell from **46% (1972) → 34% (2018)**
- 2023–24 Pew poll: 34% — consistent with the GSS decline
- Demographic gaps are sharp:
  - White Americans: **40%** trust others
  - Black Americans: **21%**
  - Hispanic Americans: **23%**
  - Lower income / lower education: significantly less trusting
- https://www.pewresearch.org/2025/05/08/americans-trust-in-one-another/

### Global Comparison — High vs. Low Income Countries (Pew, 2025) ✓
- Median trust in 16 high-income countries: **59%** say most people can be trusted
- Median trust in 9 middle-income countries: **27%**
- https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2025/12/01/where-most-people-trust-others-and-where-they-dont-around-the-world/

## Edelman Trust Barometer (2025) ✓

### The Grievance Economy
- **61% of people globally** have a moderate or high sense of grievance — believing government and business serve narrow interests and the wealthy benefit unfairly
- Those with high grievance **distrust all institutions**: business, government, media, NGOs
- The percentage who **fear government officials lie to them** rose by ~10 points between 2019 and 2024
- Only **36%** of people globally believe the next generation will be better off
- https://www.edelman.com/trust/2025/trust-barometer

### Trust Index by Country
- Most trusting nations: China (77), Indonesia (76), India (75), UAE (72)
- Least trusting: **Japan (37), Germany (41), UK (43), USA (47), France (48)**
- Five of the 10 largest economies are among the *least trusting* nations on Earth
- Trust inequality (gap between high and low income within a country) exists in **22 countries**

### One Bright Spot
- "My employer" remains the **most trusted institution** globally — 75% trust their employer
- Local and personal trust is holding up better than institutional trust

## UN World Social Report (2024) ✓

- **Over half the global population** has little or no trust in their government
- Trust levels have been declining **from one generation to the next** — a systemic pattern
- Factors driving decline: inequality, corruption, political instability, misinformation
- The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated existing erosion
- The report calls for rebuilding around **equity, economic security, and solidarity**
- https://social.desa.un.org/issues/world-social-report/TPsocialcohesion

## Key Patterns

- **The wealthy world trusts institutions least** — but middle-income countries trust each other least interpersonally
- **Younger generations trust less** than older ones — the pattern is generational, not just cyclical
- **Grievance is now the dominant emotional register** globally — 61% feel the system is rigged against them
- **Local trust (employer, neighbour) is more resilient** than national or institutional trust
- Trust and **human connection are deeply linked** — people who trust others are more likely to have close friendships, participate civically, and report better health
